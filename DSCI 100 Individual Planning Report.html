Data Description

The players.csv dataset is a list of data about players, containing 196 observations and 7 variables.

  1. experience
    • the player's previous experiences in minecraft
  2. subscribe
    • whether the player is subscribed to a game-related newsletter
  3. hashedEmail
    • the player's email in an encrypted format
  4. played_hours
    • the amount of hours the player has played on Minecraft
  5. name
    • the player's name
  6. gender
    • the player's recorded gender
  7. age
    • recorded age of the player

The sessions.csv dataset is a list of data of individual play sessions by each player, containing 1535 observations and 5 variables.

  1. hashedEmail
    • the player's email in an encrypted format
  2. start_time
    • the exact starting time of the game session
  3. end_time
    • the exact ending time of the game session
  4. original_start_time
    • start time of the game session in relation to POSIX
  5. name
    • end time of the game session in relation to POSIX

There may be some discrepencies in the data with actual facts, as the data is completely reliant on accurate and truthful reporting of data when it comes to each player. There is no guarantee that the players were all completely honest about their details.

Question

Broad Question: What player characteristics and behaviours are most predictive of subscribing to a game-related newsletter, and how do these features differ between various player types?

Specific question: Can age of player and hours played predict whether or not a player is subscribed to a game-related newsletter in the players.csv dataset?

By wrangling the data and selecting to focus on only the age, played_hours, and subscribe variables, I will be able apply a k-nn classification on the data to use for my prediction. By plotting the data, I will also be able to easily visualize any trends in the data.

Methods and Plan

To address my question of interest, I plan on performing a k-nn classification on the players dataset by using the age and played_hours variables. This method is appropriate becuase the output value/predicted variable that I am aiming for is not a numerical value, but rather a class of TRUE or FALSE, depending on if the player is subscribed or not. To apply k-nn classification, I will assume that the data provided is an accurate depiction of the types of players. To apply the model, I will first scale and center the data. Then, I will split the data into training and testing sets, and use the training set to perform a 5-fold cross-validation to determine the best k-value. I will also use the training set to create a classifier for predicting subsciption, which will then be used on the testing set to determine the accuracy of the prediction. Thus, we would be able to predict what types of players, in terms of age and play time, would be subscribed to the game newsletter. A potential issue for this model would be the outliers in the data and the large variability of factors that would affect a player's tendancy to subscribe to the newsletter. Classification may also struggle with imbalanced data, favoring majority classes or data points with long ranges. Thus, it is important to scale and standardize the data, and tune k using cross validation.
